**Title**: Collision-free Motion Planning for Robot Manipulator in Dynamic Environments Basedon Deep Reinforcement Learning

**Description**: Recently, the intersection of deep reinforcement learning (DRL) and robotics
has piqued much attention, since it enables robots to autonomously learn optimal policies
for versatile tasks. Such policies can adapt to diverse and unpredictable environments. Current studies have solved the collision-free motion planning for robot manipulators instructured environments. However, in a dynamic environment (with moving or changingobstacles), the motion planning for a robot manipulator is still challenging. In this project,
we aim to design a DRL-based motion planner that enables robots to work in dynamic
environments. Key considerations include state representation of the environment, rewardfunction design, algorithm optimization, and so on. Students will have the chance to publishin top-tier journals/conferences. Students are expected to have a basic understanding of
robot kinematics, python, and neural networks.
